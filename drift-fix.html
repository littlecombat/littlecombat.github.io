drift fix

The Nintendo Switch Joy Con controller, while a unique and arguably revolutionary design in terms of console portability, was developed with a myriad of problems that has plagued the Nintendo playerbase with frustration ever since the console was launched. The most notorious of these problems is referred to as “joystick drift” — a phenomenon that occurs when the internal components of the joystick wear down, and the sensors begin to read input that registers beyond the deadzone of the controller.

In other words, the controllers move when you don’t want them to.

Fixing drift issues in Joy Con controllers involves replacing components inside the shell itself. All of my repairs include new parts, callibration, and routine interior cleaning to ensure long-lasting playability. If you are also having issues with your Joy Con contollers staying secured to your console, I am happy to replace your plastic controller locks with durable metal locks for an additional fee.
